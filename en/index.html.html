What is Compound Interest?

Compound interest is a financial phenomenon where the interest earned is added to the principal amount and continues to earn interest itself. In simple terms - it's "interest on interest."

Unlike simple interest, which is calculated only on the original amount, compound interest causes investments to grow at an accelerated rate over time. The longer the investment period, the stronger the effect of compound interest.
